02

Our fonts.

We use three fonts. All of them are free and built into Canva — just search for them by name.

Fraunces
For headlines and titles. The big bold stuff.

A village of families.

Inter
For paragraphs and regular sentences.

This is what regular body text looks like. Clean and easy to read. Use it whenever you have a sentence or two to explain something.

DM Mono
For tiny labels and tags. Use sparingly.

★ Save the date ★

★ Pro tip

If your tool doesn't have one of these fonts, the closest replacements are: instead of Fraunces, try Playfair Display or DM Serif Display. Instead of Inter, try Arial or Helvetica. Instead of DM Mono, try Courier or any monospaced font.

03

Our look.

A few things to remember when you're putting something together. These little choices make everything feel like it belongs together.

Hi there

One italic, pink word per headline

Pick one word to be pink and italic in your headline — usually the most important or playful word. Just one!

Thick black borders

Cards, buttons, and shapes get a thick black outline (about 3 pixels). It makes things feel friendly and printed.

Hard shadows (not blurry)

If you add a shadow, make it solid black and offset down and to the right. No fuzzy edges. Looks like a sticker.

★

Stars and circles for decoration

Sprinkle in pink stars or solid colored circles in the corners. Don't overdo it — usually 2 or 3 per design.

04

How it sounds.

When you're writing words for a flyer or post, here's the vibe we're going for.

Yes, like this
  • "We're a community of EGR families."
  • "Phone-free bowling night — pizza included."
  • "Stronger together."
  • Use everyday words and contractions ("we're," "it's").
Not like this
  • "Empower your child's digital wellness journey."
  • "Don't be the parent who…"
  • Scary statistics with no context.
  • Lots of exclamation points or all caps.
05

If in doubt.

When you're not sure if something looks right, ask yourself:

  • → Did I use one of our 5 colors? (Not random other ones)
  • → Is there one italic, pink word in the headline?
  • → Do the shapes have thick black borders?
  • → Does the writing sound warm and human, not like marketing?
